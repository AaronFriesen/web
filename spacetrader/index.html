Space Trader

Fall 2012, I worked on a team with Alexander Ikonomidis, Randal Brown, and Ashu Gupta on a game called Space Trader for CS2340. We made the entire game from the ground up, working as a team for 4 months. We actually did such a good job and went so overboard with additional features, we currently hold the class record of 236% on the final project.

The game's title screen.

The game puts the player in the character of (you guessed it) a space trader. The goal is to fly around the galaxy, buying and selling goods between various solar systems. The player can upgrade their ship with various enhancements to allow them to do things such as hold more cargo, travel faster, and even defend against rogue pirates. There are random encounters as the player travels across the galaxy which tip them in on the state of other systems. See that a system is experiencing a drought? Fill up your cargo bay with water and fly over for huge profits!

There are quite a few systems to travel to, each with multiple planets of different types and governments. I did the majority of the backend programming, in particular with modeling the planets, systems, and encounters. Alex worked on a lot of the GUI programming with Ashu, and Randal and I were the team designers and debuggers. Overall the project was a lot of fun and was good teamworking experience.
