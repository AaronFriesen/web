About

My name is Aaron Friesen, and I'm a 3rd year Computer Science student. I've been attending the Georgia Institute of Technology since Fall of 2011, and intend on graduating in the Spring of 2015. My hometown is a little town in North Georgia called Woodstock - not the one from the concert, I'm afraid. My passion has been video games for as long as I can remember, and I'm making it my mission to break into the game industry. In addition to video games, I spend a lot of time playing card games, board games, and tabletop games for a couple reasons:

1. I think they're fun!

2. I think there are a lot of things that each medium has to learn from each other.

Some of my favorite games include League of Legends, Stepmania, Dota 2, Good ol' Warcraft 3, Magic: The Gathering (though I tend to play just Limited formats these days), Pathfinder, Mage Knight, and the Fire Emblem series. My favorite game, bar none, has to go to Fire Emblem: Awakening. The story is fantastic and the gameplay covers the entire spectrum from easy to mind-bogglingly impossible. The only thing the game is missing is a strong multiplayer mechanic (what I wouldn't give to play against people's teams head-to-head), though the Streetpass team share mechanic does fill in for that somewhat. I could go on for days about FE:A. Long story short: if you enjoy strategy games, play it. It's fantastic.

I've been running Pathfinder games for about 5 years now and I enjoy it quite a bit. Being a DM really helps get the creative juices flowing, so to speak. They let you create a world and collectively write a story with your players. My most recent games include a somewhat standard game in a custom setting - it mostly revolved around subverting an evil Elf Queen who was attempting to resurrect an ancient world-eating monster. I also co-ran a game where the players were all Greek demigods coming into their own, finding out about their godly heritage and encountering famous Greek mythos such as The Fates and Hephaestus.

Recently I've been very interested in Hex, one of the first MMOTCGs out there. It's essentially a Hearthstone competitor with some neat RPG elements as well, such as multiplayer dungeons with boss enemies. I applied to their Cryptozoic Apprentice program for game design and made it quite far in the interview process - all told, I ended up creating about 20 pages of card designs and answering quite a few Game Theory questions. I learned quite a bit from the experience and I think it's made me understand a lot more about how games are made. I'm still looking for an internship for the summer of 2014, but I'm confident that my newly-honed game design skills will get me where I want to be.
