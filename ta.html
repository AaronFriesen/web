My Work

Undergraduate Teaching Assistant

Spring 2012, I started work as an Undergraduate Teaching Assistant (TA) for Georgia Tech's CS 1331, Introduction to Object Oriented Programming. It covers in-depth Java programming as well as object-oriented programming principles and design. I taught weekly recitations to 50 people per semester, as well as held office hours for one-on-one student help. I was also responsible for grading homeworks and tests on a regular basis. I was a TA for 4 semesters today before I was promoted to be the Head TA.

Me teaching a lecture on sorting algorithms. I like to have audience participation whenever I can.

As the Head TA, I was in charge of managing the other TAs. I made sure that everyone knew what they were doing on a weekly basis as well as coordinated their office hours and grading. I delegated responsibility for writing homeworks to the TAs, though I also write a fair number myself. I wrote weekly recitation guides so the TAs could all consistently teach to GT standards. I was the Head TA for CS 1331 for 3 semesters and it was a very rewarding experience.

This lecture, I had students come up to the front so they could physically see how each algorithm behaves.

Being the Head TA was about 20 hours of work per week, between scheduling TA duties, working on homeworks, and communicating with students. It was a non-trivial amount of work, but it has been some of the most enjoyable work I've done so far. Some examples of lectures I have given:
